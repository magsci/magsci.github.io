Theory



The main equations:


Steady state:
\[\left(1-\alpha(T)\right)Q=\epsilon\sigma T^4\]


Time evolution:
\[C\frac{dT}{dt}=\left(1-\alpha\right)Q-\epsilon\sigma T^4\]




The steady state equation has on the left hand side the incident radiant energy less the fraction that is reflected. This is set equal to the outgoing longwave energy which is on the right hand side. In the case of the time evolution equation the change in temperature is caused by a difference between the incident and outgoing radiation. The left hand side of this equation is the overall heat capacity of the earth multiplied by the change of temperature per unit time (namely the change in energy).


The model for albedo-ice feedback has used the value of e as 1.0. This is only the case for a perfect black body. The n layer theory of the atmosphere (namely the Earth which is not a perfect black body) as described by Dessler (2012) gives the temperature as follows

\[T=\sqrt[4]{\frac{(n+1)S(1-\alpha)}{4\sigma}}\]

with an inferred n of 0.65 when observed values are used This leads to a value of e = 0.605 when

\[e=\frac{1}{(n+1)}\]

This value of e =0.605 is also supported by Budd (2018)

Time evolution with feedback:


\[C\frac{dT}{dt}=\left(1-\alpha(T)\right)Q-\epsilon\sigma T^4\]

How alpha could depend on temperature:


\[\alpha(T)=0.495-0.205y\]

\[y=\tanh\left(\kappa \left(T-T_{c}\right) \right)\]


\[\kappa = 0.133 \hspace{10pt}and\hspace{10pt} T_{c}=275K\]


Alternatively :
\[\alpha(T)=0.7-0.4\frac{e^\frac{(T-265)}{5}}{1 + e^\frac{(T-265)}{5}}\]

Or a linear regression such as :
\[\alpha(T)=a + bT\] where a and b are constants
